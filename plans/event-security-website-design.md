## 2. Common Design Patterns Across All Sites

After visiting and analyzing 15+ competitor websites, these patterns emerged consistently:

### 2.1 Navigation & Layout

- **Sticky header with mega-menu:** Almost every major site (BEST, Allied Universal, Securitas, G4S, Showsec, Stadium) uses a sticky top nav with dropdown mega-menus that reveal service categories on hover.
- **Hero section with dual CTA buttons:** Typically "Get a Quote" / "Contact Sales" alongside "Learn More" or "Explore Services" — the pattern is two buttons, one primary (contact/quote), one secondary (explore).
- **Trust bar immediately below hero:** Logos of well-known clients (NFL, MLB, Premier League clubs, Live Nation) displayed as a horizontal strip. This is universal across CSC, BEST, Showsec, Stadium, and G4S.
- **Footer with 4–5 columns:** Services, Solutions, Company, Resources, Contact — plus accreditation badges (ISO, SIA, DHS Safety Act).

### 2.2 Homepage Structure

The homepage follows a remarkably consistent template across nearly all companies:

1. **Hero section** — Full-width background image (crowd/event/stadium), headline about safety/trust, 2 CTAs
2. **Trust bar** — Client logos (sports teams, venues, event organizers)
3. **Service cards** — 3–6 icon/image cards linking to service pages
4. **Stats/counter section** — Numbers (events served, staff deployed, years experience)
5. **Venue/event types carousel** — Images with labels (sporting, concerts, corporate, etc.)
6. **Resources/case studies** — 3-card grid linking to articles or success stories
7. **Final CTA section** — "Talk to an expert" or "Get a quote"

### 2.3 Color Schemes

- **Deep blues and navy** — Dominant across CSC, BEST, Allied Universal, Securitas, G4S. Blue communicates trust, authority, security.
- **Black/dark gray** — Showsec, Stadium, G4S use dark headers and footers.
- **Accent colors** — Yellow/gold (Showsec, BEST), red (Securitas red dots), green (Stadium green logo).
- **White space** — Heavy use of white backgrounds with dark text for readability.

### 2.4 Typography

- **Sans-serif exclusively** — All sites use clean sans-serif fonts (Open Sans, Lato, Montserrat, or custom).
- **Heavy bold headlines** — Big, bold (700–900 weight) hero headlines.
- **Body text at 16–18px** — Readable body copy.
- **All-caps section headers** — Used in navigation and section titles for a professional feel.

### 2.5 Imagery

- **Real photography over illustrations** — Authentic photos of uniformed staff at actual events.
- **Diverse, smiling staff** — Emphasis on friendly, approachable security personnel.
- **Aerial/wide shots of crowds** — Conveys scale and capability.
- **Behind-the-scenes operations** — Control rooms, briefings, planning meetings.
- **No stock-photo feel** — The best sites use genuine event photography.

### 2.6 Content Patterns

- **"Trusted by" language** — EVERY site uses "Trusted by" or "Proud partner of" language.
- **Case studies as social proof** — Typically 3–6 case studies with client logos and quotes.
- **FAQ accordions** — Common on service pages to address objections.
- **Values/Mission section** — Showsec, Stadium, and BEST prominently display their mission/vision/values.

## 3. Unique Differentiators from Individual Sites

### 3.1 CSC (csc-usa.com) — "The Gold Standard"

- **Differentiator:** Numbers-driven credibility. Leads with "60,000 trained team members," "33 Super Bowls," "10 Olympic Games."
- **Design takeaway:** Lead with quantified experience prominently. Use a "Gold Standard" positioning.
- **What to borrow:** The "DHS Safety Act Certified" badge placement. The bold stat counters.

### 3.2 BEST Crowd Management (best.garda.com) — End-to-End Clarity

- **Differentiator:** Three clear service pillars: Event Security, Event Staffing, Event Consulting. Very clear segmentation.
- **Design takeaway:** Service cards with distinct icons and clear 1-line descriptions. The "150+ venues, 500+ partnerships" stat bar.
- **What to borrow:** The way they separate "Services" from "Solutions" (by event type). Excellent resources section with blog/case study mix.

### 3.3 Allied Universal (aus.com) — Enterprise Authority

- **Differentiator:** Scale and comprehensiveness. "750K workforce," "400+ Fortune 500 clients."
- **Design takeaway:** The mega-menu is best-in-class for organizing many service lines. The "Security in our Solutions" tagline is memorable.
- **What to borrow:** The partnership spotlight (Seattle Mariners, BMO Stadium) — using featured client stories on the event services page.

### 3.4 Securitas (securitasinc.com) — Tech-Forward Positioning

- **Differentiator:** Strong focus on technology integration (MySecuritas app, remote monitoring, risk intelligence).
- **Design takeaway:** The tabbed content (Overview, Benefits, Solutions, FAQ, Related Content) is excellent for deep service pages. FAQ accordion with detailed answers.
- **What to borrow:** The "People + Technology" framing. The detailed FAQ approach.

### 3.5 Showsec (showsec.co.uk) — Values-Led Branding

- **Differentiator:** Strong mission/vision/values displayed prominently. "Integrity, Responsibility, Solution-finding."
- **Design takeaway:** The homepage includes latest job opportunities — treating careers as a key conversion. Clean, unfussy design.
- **What to borrow:** Careers section on homepage. Values display. Simple, confident copywriting.

### 3.6 Stadium (wearestadium.com) — Sector Specialization

- **Differentiator:** Splits services by sector (Arena, Sports, Conference, Crowd Mgmt, Traffic Mgmt, Training). The HVM rental offering is a unique differentiator.
- **Design takeaway:** Beautiful use of client logos in testimonial quotes. The "What we do" 3-column breakdown. Video background on hero.
- **What to borrow:** The "Subscribe to newsletter" callout. The accreditation bars (ISO, SIA, etc.).

### 3.7 G4S (g4s.com) — Scale + Safety/Service/Security Triangle

- **Differentiator:** Three-pillar framework: Safety, Security, Service. Clear "how we deliver" visual.
- **Design takeaway:** The capability breakdown (Event Fixtures, Concerts, Conferences, Government, Stadium) is comprehensive yet scannable.
- **What to borrow:** The Safety/Security/Service triangle as a positioning framework.

### 3.8 Achilleus Security (achilleus-security.co.uk) — Niche Focus & Testimonials

- **Differentiator:** Football/sports specialty with heavy testimonial focus. "We Go Further To Protect You."
- **Design takeaway:** Testimonial carousel is the most prominent on any site — quotes from Wembley, QPR, Twickenham. The threat level indicator in header is unique.
- **What to borrow:** Heavy testimonial usage with named individuals and roles. "Get in touch now for obligation-free advice."

### 3.9 Stadium People (stadiumpeople.com) — "Guest Experience" Focus

- **Differentiator:** Positions as reinventing crowd management and guest experience. Tagline: "Give your guest the best."
- **Design takeaway:** Very simple three-button homepage (Jobs, Hire, Employee Support). Single-page feel.
- **What to borrow:** The staff-centric approach (three equal CTAs for job seekers, employers, and employees).

### 3.10 Crowd Safety (crowdsafety.org) — Consultancy Authority

- **Differentiator:** Expert witness and training focus. "50M+ safe audience members, 30+ years, 48 countries."
- **Design takeaway:** Stats-driven credibility. Clean, minimalist design.
- **What to borrow:** The "Brands our leadership has provided services for" logo strip.

## 4. Specific Design Recommendations for Seguras

### 4.1 Brand Positioning

Based on the competitor landscape, Seguras should position itself as:

> **"Portugal's most trusted event security partner — combining local expertise with international standards."**

The gap in the market: No existing Portuguese event security company has a modern, professional web presence that matches the UK/US leaders. Seguras can leapfrog local competitors by adopting the design patterns of Showsec, BEST, and G4S while emphasizing:

- **Local knowledge** — Deep understanding of Portuguese venues, regulations, and culture
- **International standards** — SIA-equivalent certifications, ISO compliance
- **Bilingual** — Portuguese + English website from day one
- **Personal service** — Agility of a smaller company with professionalism of a global firm

### 4.2 Recommended Tech Stack

- **Framework:** Next.js (for SEO, performance, and image optimization)
- **CMS:** Sanity or Contentful (for case studies, news, careers content)
- **Hosting:** Vercel (for global CDN, especially for Portuguese/European audiences)
- **Analytics:** Plausible or Fathom (privacy-focused, important for EU)
- **Cookie consent:** Cookiebot or Osano (GDPR compliance)

### 4.3 Color Palette

```
Primary:      Deep Navy      #0A1628  (trust, authority, professionalism)
Secondary:    Safety Yellow   #F5C518  (energy, visibility, confidence)
Accent:       Signal Red      #E63946  (urgency, alerts, CTAs, sparingly)
Neutral Dark: Charcoal        #1D1D1F  (text, headings)
Neutral Mid:  Slate           #6B7280  (body text)
Neutral Light: Cloud          #F3F4F6  (backgrounds, cards)
White:        Pure White      #FFFFFF
```

**Why this palette:**
- Navy blue is the industry standard — it signals trust and authority instantly
- Safety yellow is used by Showsec and BEST as an accent — it evokes warning vests, safety gear, and high visibility
- Signal red for CTAs only — red attracts attention but should be used sparingly to avoid alarm
- The overall feel is professional, trustworthy, with energetic accents

### 4.4 Typography

- **Headings:** Inter (sans-serif, geometric, excellent legibility) — weights 700–900
- **Body:** Inter (same family for consistency) — weight 400, size 16–18px
- **Navigation:** Inter Medium, 15px, letter-spacing 0.5px (all-caps for section headers optional)
- **Tagline/FEatured text:** Optional use of a serif like Playfair Display for contrast on hero headlines only

### 4.5 Photography Direction

**DO shoot:**

- Uniformed Seguras staff at Portuguese venues (Estádio da Luz, Estádio José Alvalade, Altice Arena, Super Bock Arena)
- Staff interacting positively with fans/crowds (smiling, helpful, approachable)
- Control room / operations center (tech-forward, professional)
- Pre-event briefings (teamwork, preparation, competence)
- Aerial crowd shots at major events
- Candid portraits of staff (diverse ages, backgrounds)

**DON'T use:**

- Aggressive-looking guards
- Dark, threatening imagery
- Generic stock photos of crowds
- Handcuffs, weapons, or militaristic imagery (this is event security, not private military)

**Photography style:** Warm, high-contrast, candid. Think editorial/documentary style. The goal is to show that security can be both professional and friendly.

## 6. Color Psychology

### Industry Context

The event security industry has a well-established visual language:

| Color | Message | Used By | Notes for Seguras |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Navy Blue** | Trust, authority, stability, professionalism | CSC, BEST, Allied Universal, Securitas, G4S | **Essential.** Primary brand color. |
| **Dark Gray/Black** | Seriousness, formality, premium | Showsec, G4S, Stadium | Use for headers and footers. |
| **Safety Yellow** | Energy, visibility, confidence, caution | BEST, Showsec (accents) | **Perfect for CTAs.** Links to safety vests — industry-recognized. |
| **White** | Cleanliness, transparency, simplicity | All | Background for readability. |
| **Red** | Urgency, importance, alerts | Securitas (dot), Achilleus (accents) | Minimal — CTAs only. |
| **Green** | Safety, growth, Portugal's national color | Stadium (brand) | Use sparingly — could reference Portugal's flag subtly. |

### Seguras-Specific Recommendation

The name "Seguras" means "safe/secure" (plural feminine) in Portuguese — this is a gift. The brand can lean into the native-language trust signal:

- **Primary:** Deep Navy (#0A1628) — "you are in safe hands"
- **Secondary:** Safety Yellow (#F5C518) — "we are visible, alert, confident"
- **Portuguese accent:** A subtle use of Portuguese green (#00843D) or red (#FF0000) could anchor the brand locally, but use very sparingly — navy + yellow is the safer industry-standard choice
- **Preferred approach:** Navy + Yellow as primary, with warm white and charcoal as neutrals

## 7. Photography Style

### Industry Standards

The best sites (Showsec, BEST, Stadium) all follow these principles:

1. **Authenticity over perfection** — Real events, real staff, real crowds
2. **Human warmth** — Staff smiling, helping, engaging with attendees
3. **Scale and energy** — Wide shots of packed stadiums, festivals, concerts
4. **Professionalism** — Uniforms crisp, staff alert, operations organized
5. **Diversity** — Age, gender, ethnicity representation

### Seguras Photography Brief

**Core photo library needs:**

| Category | Examples | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Staff portraits** | Uniformed stewards at venue entrances, smiling | Humanize the brand |
| **Crowd management** | Staff guiding crowds, managing queues | Demonstrate capability |
| **Operations** | Control room, team briefing, equipment | Show professionalism |
| **Event coverage** | Sporting, concerts, corporate (from real events) | Provide proof |
| **Team culture** | Staff training, team building, behind-the-scenes | Recruitment appeal |

**Photo do's and don'ts:**

- ✅ Candid, natural lighting (editorial style)
- ✅ Warm color grading (not cold/clinical)
- ✅ First-person perspective (show what the staff see)
- ❌ No aggressive postures or expressions
- ❌ No lone guards in dark corners (creates fear)
- ❌ No obvious stock photography

**Budget tip:** Hire a documentary/event photographer for 2-3 actual events. This will yield authentic images far superior to stock photos and can be used across the entire site.

## 8. Tone of Voice

### Industry Analysis

| Company | Tone | Example Phrase |
|---|---|---|
| BEST | Professional, confident, trustworthy | "Trusted security services that protect people and the event experience" |
| Showsec | Calm, authoritative, values-driven | "To act with integrity, take responsibility and find solutions" |
| Stadium | Passionate, dedicated, service-oriented | "We keep spectators and participants safe" |
| G4S | Reassuring, comprehensive, partner-like | "We create a security solution which protects what is most important to you" |
| CSC | Proud, accomplished, gold-standard | "The Gold Standard in Crowd Management" |
| Achilleus | Direct, personal, family-feel | "We Go Further To Protect You" |

### Seguras Tone of Voice Guidelines

**Personality:** Professional but warm. Authoritative but approachable. Portuguese-rooted but internationally-minded.

**Guiding principles:**

| Principle | In practice |
|---|---|
| **Confident, not arrogant** | "Temos a experiência que o seu evento merece" (We have the experience your event deserves) — not "We're the best" |
| **Warm, not casual** | Professional Portuguese with a human touch. Use "você" not "tu" |
| **Clear, not jargon-heavy** | Avoid excessive security terminology. Explain in plain language |
| **Reassuring, not alarming** | Focus on safety and enjoyment, not threats and dangers |
| **Local, not provincial** | Proudly Portuguese but designed to serve international clients |

**Tagline options:**

- *"Segurança que inspira confiança"* — Security that inspires confidence
- *"Proteger pessoas é a nossa missão"* — Protecting people is our mission
- *"O seu evento. A nossa responsabilidade."* — Your event. Our responsibility.
- *"Seguras. Como o nome diz."* — Seguras. As the name says. (Clever wordplay)

**Recommended:** *"Segurança que inspira confiança"* (Security that Inspires Confidence) — works in both Portuguese and English contexts.

## 10. Conversion Optimization

### 10.1 Primary Conversion Goals

| Goal | Page | Metric |
|---|---|---|
| Get a quote request | Homepage, Services pages | Form submissions |
| Phone call | All pages | Click-to-call tracking |
| Newsletter signup | Homepage, Blog | Email signups |
| Job applications | Careers page | Applications submitted |
| Case study downloads | Case study pages | PDF downloads / views |

### 10.2 Proven Conversion Patterns (from competitors)

**Pattern 1: The "No-Obligation" Frame**
- Achilleus uses "obligation-free advice" — removes pressure
- Seguras should use: "Orçamento sem compromisso" (No-obligation quote)

**Pattern 2: Response Time Guarantee**
- None of the competitors explicitly state response time — this is an opportunity
- Seguras can differentiate: "Respondemos em até 24 horas" (We reply within 24 hours)

**Pattern 3: Multi-Channel Contact**
- Every competitor shows phone number + contact form + often live chat
- Seguras needs: Phone (top right sticky), Contact form, WhatsApp (essential for Portugal)

**Pattern 4: Progressive Disclosure Form**
- Don't ask everything upfront — start with "Name, Email, Event Type" → then expand
- Use multi-step form with progress indicator

**Pattern 5: Quote Form on Every Page**
- BEST has "TALK TO AN EXPERT" sticky on every page
- Seguras should have a floating "Pedir Orçamento" button

### 10.3 CTA Strategy

| Placement | Copy (PT) | Copy (EN) | Design |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero (primary) | "Pedir Orçamento" | "Get a Quote" | Yellow filled button |
| Hero (secondary) | "Os Nossos Serviços" | "Our Services" | Outline button |
| Service cards | "Saber Mais" | "Learn More" | Text link with arrow |
| Case studies | "Ler Caso" | "Read Case Study" | Text link |
| Bottom of pages | "Solicitar Orçamento" | "Request a Quote" | Yellow filled |
| Floating button | "Orçamento Rápido" | "Quick Quote" | Fixed bottom-right |
| Careers | "Candidatar-se" | "Apply Now" | Primary button |

### 10.4 Form Optimization

- **Minimum fields initially:** Name, Email, Phone, Event Type
- **Conditional fields:** Show "Expected Attendance" only after Event Type is selected
- **GDPR checkbox:** Required, pre-checked (for Portuguese market)
- **CAPTCHA:** Use invisible reCAPTCHA — don't hurt conversion
- **Confirmation:** Thank-you page with next steps + estimated response time
- **Auto-responder:** Email confirmation with case study PDF download link

## 11. Trust-Building Elements

### 11.1 Required Trust Signals (from competitor analysis)

| Signal | Where | Source inspiration |
|---|---|---|
| Client logos | Homepage hero → trust bar | EVERY competitor does this |
| Stat counters | Above the fold on homepage | CSC, BEST, G4S, Showsec |
| Testimonials with names | Homepage, About, Services | Achilleus (best example) |
| Case studies with results | Dedicated page | BEST, Stadium |
| Certification/accreditation badges | Footer + About page | Stadium, G4S, CSC |
| Team photos | About page | Showsec, Stadium |
| Media coverage/"As seen in" | Homepage or About | BEST |
| GDPR/privacy assurance | Contact form + Footer | Legal requirement |
| Physical address | Footer | ALL competitors |
| Real phone number | Header sticky | ALL competitors |

### 11.2 Seguras-Specific Trust Strategy

**Phase 1 (Launch):**
- Professional website design (this guide)
- Real office address in Lisbon/Porto
- Registered company with public registry number (NIPC)
- Professional photography
- 3-5 solid testimonials from early clients
- Certifications display (PSP licensing, insurance)

**Phase 2 (6 months):**
- 5-10 published case studies
- Blog content (10+ articles on event safety in Portugal)
- Google Reviews profile with 10+ reviews
- Industry association memberships (APSEI - Portuguese Security Association)
- Video testimonials from clients

**Phase 3 (12 months):**
- Media mentions / PR
- Award entries
- Client logo strip with recognizable names
- Video tour of operations
- "Behind the scenes" content series

### 11.3 The "Trust Gap" Opportunity

Most event security websites focus heavily on **what** they do, not **who** they are. The opportunity for Seguras is:

- **Show the people** — Team photos, staff spotlights, founder story
- **Show the process** — Behind-the-scenes of risk assessment, planning, event day operations
- **Show the results** — Quantified outcomes (zero incidents, 99% client satisfaction, etc.)
- **Be transparent** — Pricing guide or range, clear service descriptions, no hidden fees
